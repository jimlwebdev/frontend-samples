INDICATIONS AND USAGE:
TRAVATAN Z® (travoprost ophthalmic solution) 0.004% is a prostaglandin analog indicated for the reduction of elevated intraocular pressure in patients with open-angle glaucoma or ocular hypertension.

Dosage and Administration:
One drop in the affected eye(s) once daily in the evening.

IMPORTANT SAFETY INFORMATION
Warnings and Precautions:
Pigmentation: Pigmentation of the iris, periorbital tissue (eyelid) and eyelashes can occur. Iris pigmentation likely to be permanent.
Eyelash Changes: Gradual change to eyelashes including increased length, thickness and number of lashes. Usually reversible.

Adverse Reactions:
Most common adverse reaction (30% to 50%) is conjunctival hyperemia.

Use In Specific Populations:
Use in pediatric patients below the age of 16 years is not recommended because of potential safety concerns related to increased pigmentation following long-term chronic use.

Each office
visit is just a
snapshot of a
patient’s IOP

IOP is generally assessed with a single measurement during daytime office hours. However, a single measurement may not accurately represent IOP over the course of a full day.3-6

A single measurement may miss IOP spikes, peaks, or elevated pressure levels that occur at other time points.3-6

In a double-masked, randomized study published in the Journal of Glaucoma, BAK-free TRAVATAN Z® (travoprost ophthalmic solution) 0.004% maintained a 30% IOP lowering from baseline1

Baseline mean IOP, mm Hg (95% CI): 12 hours post, 27.0 (0.2, -0.6), N=322; 14 hours post, 25.5 (0.3, -0.6), N=322; 20 hours post, 24.8 (0.3, -0.5), N=322.

Week 2 mean IOP, mm Hg (95% CI): 12 hours post, 18.5 (0.4, -0.5), N=320; 14 hours post, 17.7 (0.4, -0.6), N=320; 20 hours post, 17.3 (0.4, -0.6), N=319.

Week 6 mean IOP, mm Hg (95% CI): 12 hours post, 18.7 (0.6, -0.4), N=313; 14 hours post, 17.8 (0.5, -0.5), N=313; 20 hours post, 17.3 (0.5, -0.5), N=309.

3 Months mean IOP, mm Hg (95% CI): 12 hours post, 18.7 (0.5, -0.4), N=309; 14 hours post, 17.7 (0.6, -0.4), N=308; 20 hours post, 17.4 (0.8, -0.2), N=310.

TRAVATAN Z® (travoprost ophthalmic solution) 0.004% sustained 30% IOP reductions in an integrated analysis of 7 randomized clinical trials with 1,563 patients7

An optimal goal may be to reduce IOP by at least 30%.8

The integrated analysis comprised data from 7 peer reviewed, published, prospective randomized trials, including the Lewis study. The 7 studies were selected based on shared characteristics. All but one were registration trials; the sole non-registration trial was a duration of action study conducted to registration trial standards.
